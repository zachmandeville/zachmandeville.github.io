The First Entry

1-16-18

This is the first blog entry for the first sprint of my first week of Dev Academy. I, excitedly, do not know what I am doing. I want to start up this blog with no expecations for style or tone. The only core value I'm holding here is honesty. And, so, honestly: I'm a little tired, and self-conscious, and so this first entry will be straightforward and short like a clearing of the throat, helping myself express my words more clearly later.

I just watched Shereef's fireside chat and the primary takeaway I got from it was relief. I knew I wanted to learn how to code, and I knew that Enspiral would likely be the best school for this, but this video was a grand reassurance that my feelings were right.

My relief comes in that I feel EDA/DBC are emphasising learning to code as a personal journey, a way to more fully express your self. The tone of the video was encouragement, saying we should allow ourselves to get the most from this adventure. It was not “Do exactly what i say and you'll have a Ferrari by this time next year.” Or, “Us coders are different from the average person, and I'm going to be as hard on you as possible to weed out the average poseurs from the truly leet.” I've heard that tone so much in tech-writing, or the tutorials I've watched online. This toxic tech-bro culture that turned me away from wanting to learn this stuff because I didn't want to become a jerk too. Shereef and EDA are showing an alternate path. Here we are watching an introductory video that essentially says, “You doing okay? Listen to your body. Be good to yourself, by expecting the most out of yourself. Let's be mindful and nice to each other.” So in terms of how I'ma approach this, it'll be with gusto and full-speed.

I also feel like this week is introducing us to this particular style of teaching, this particularly vulnerable tone, more than any language or technology. We are being told about git, and the command line, and HTML, but we're being told in a specific way that I'm starting to see is deliberate, and intentional. That deliberate pace I wasn't necessarily expecting before this week, but I gotta say I like it!

That's all, this is all good!

Current Mood: giddy.
