c7 Motivation

How does Life look from the 'pleasure, passion, purpose' lens?

Honestly: pretty good! This is something I've believed in for a long time, and have spent a lot of personal time figuring out how to be patient with passions if they are fulfilling a larger purpose. In other word: the things I'm doing may not make me immediately (or ever) famous or rich, but if they are important and fit my higher purpose, then it won't matter. I am fortunate to have found passions that put me into the flow easily-- and coding itself is one of them. I don't believe in denying or restricting pleasure-- the world is meant to be celebrated and enjoyed! But I've learned to check in and ask, "does this actually feel good, or does it just appear to feel good?" This helps me figure out what is good and in the flow, and what's a depressive inertia disguising itself as a pleasure. This distinction requires continual check-ins, there is no dogmatic separation from the pleasure, passion, and purpose to me which is why meditation and mindfulness are so important.

What are your core values?

My core values would be kindness, empathy, pleasure, magic, and the continual exploration and expansion of metaphor. I think that magic is real, and you should draw it out and celebrate it in all parts of your world. A way to do this is through kindness to one another, and your world, and an empathy to not only listen to other people, but to your heart and to your surroundings and what they are asking for too. And I think that none of this matters if you aren't happy like a bee drenched in pollen. I believe that the world is as we describe it, and that there is an element to this world that we can't describe, which is why it's powerful. We strive to explain this element as closely as we can, to use comparisons and descriptions to sort of describe around the thing, to better hint at it's shape. And so continually expanding the words that you can speak can help you better articulate that essence that drives you. The counterside of this is that denying people the ability to speak, or blocking the entrances to learn more languages and words, is cosmically criminal.

What do you stand for?

I stand for equality, endless generosity, the power of the intuition and the irrational, and the need to bring more voices and views into the technology that drives so much of our world. Computers should augment our relationships and capabilities, and not limit or control them. And so computer technology is a language more of us should have the ability to speak and alter.

How did you find the resilience and meditation exercise?

Really good, and interesting! I found it super easy to recall and live in a failure, even though it was unpleasant. But when I experienced the failure as physioligical changes, they seemed just passing and funny. Like, my shoulders siezed up and it felt like there were worms crawling in my chest...but once you say 'oh, it feels like lil worms crawling across my chest.' you realize how easy of a feeling that is to experience. It's a far easier feeling than an upset stomach, or even something inconsequential like too-spicy food or a foot that's asleep. And so once the body feelings felt small, that failure felt small and passing too.

I had a harder time staying in the moment of a success. I could think of one, but I immediately felt silly just thinking about it. So I had to really sit during those two minutes and be in that moment, even though the moment was a good one? The body feeling was one of intense(but happy) restlessness. I wanted to stand up, and I really wanted to speak or shout. It felt like a ball of electricity moving up and down my body at increasing speed--which is pleasant, but not for a sustained period and I really wanted to let that ball out.
