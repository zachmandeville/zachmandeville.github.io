What is a responsive site, and why is it important?

A responsive site is one that 'responds' to the screen-size of the visitor of the site, and reacts accordingly. And so it is optimized for phones, and desktop, and tablet, and so on. There isn't one particular type of browser or device needed to view your webpage. This is important, as it means you are not limiting your potential audience by something arbitrary like access to a computer.

What's Mobile First Design, and why is it important>

Mobile first means designing your site for phone screens first, and then using media queries(if needed) for optimization on desktop. It encourages you to make sure the contents of your site are accessible even within the most restricted screen, and then allows you to expand from there. This will make it easier in the future to further expand and optimize your site. By doing it for computers first, you will have a harder job trying to then scale your grand vision down to a phone.

What is a framework, and what are the pros and cons?

A framework is a full CSS stylesheet that you can reference on your own site. It allows you to not have to worry about the elemental stuff of your site, as these details have already been worked out within the framework. You do have to learn the framework, though, and how to reference these elemental stylings. The pro would be speed and consistency. You can build out a site fairly easily by referencing pre-made styles. The cons are that you have to learn the docs, instead of learning css--in addition, you'll be potentially making a site that looks like anyone else's site made within that framework. That's an issue I had with bootstrap even before I knew what exactly it was. I could tell a bootstrap site without looking at the code, based purely on aesthetics. So the speed and consistency can also lead to boring sameness.

What is a wireframe, and why do we use it?

A wireframe is a sketch of how you'd like your site to look, using the "boxifying" philosophy of web design. It's a nice way to gather your thoughts around the general structure of your site, what content you'd like it to have, and how you'd like folks to navigate this content. Wireframes also help visualize how the site will look on mobile and then extend into desktops.
