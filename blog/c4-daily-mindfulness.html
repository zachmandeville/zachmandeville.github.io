What did you learn from the mindfulness conversation?

o

I used the mindfulness convo with my fiancee on the morning of our wedding, and lemme tell you it HELPED. We had a swirl of family around us, and things that needed to be done for the day to go well. We'd intentially designed our wedding to be as stress-free as possible, but there was still a lot of coordination and planning we needed to do throughout the day. So in the morning we did a mindful conversation. She had 3 minutes to talk about her hopes for the day, her anxieties, what she needed, etc. Then I spoke back to her what I understood from what she said, and she clarified until we were on the same page. Then I did the same thing. What happened, then, is we had a better understanding of what exactly we needed to reduce stress, and the signals we'd send each other when we needed help. But we also started the day saying "I am wanting to listen to you and help you" and just that intention helped.

Any other musings?

I am falling into the groove of Meng's tone more, and one thing that I like about his "MINDHACK" technique is the notion of exercising. In meditation, when your mind drifts and you bring it back, it's not a failure because of the drifting. Instead, you just engaged in the exercise of bringing back focus. I like that approach, as it keeps the whole experience positive and an act of learning, not some thing you have to master.
