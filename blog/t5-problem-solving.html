My cool Problem Solving Story

Hello, non-tech friend! Lemme tell you about the time I added a custom exercise section to a "yoga helper" I made. This helper went through different poses and set a timer for how long you should do each one. I wanted a way to put in new routines into ths helper and have it understand them and run them. I knew that I didn't want this to be especially complicated, and had a dream that people could write out routines as like plaintext files to share with one another. I had no clue how to accomplish this.

To solve the problem I did a mixture of "pseudocode" and "ask a friend". I wrote up a user story of sorts, describing how I'd want someone to be able to write up a routine, give it to the program, and have it run properly--and that the routine was separate from the program. Then, I went to a weekly computer club and asked a friend for advice. Since I'd worked out the general process, I was able to articulate where I was still confused and received better help. My friend said to take a look at .ini files.

I then searched for more info on .ini files and found out how they're written, and how they could be read by a program. These work elegantly for when you're trying to have a user put in different sorts of configurations. Why I loved this solution, is that this concpet could be used for any number of other programs too. And so I learned another type of standard that could be used by multiple languages or devices, that solved my yoga app problem, and other problems down the road!
